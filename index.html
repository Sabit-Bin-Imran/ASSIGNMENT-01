Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

creating new ideas, products, services,
or methods that bring improvements
or solve problems in novel ways.

Sustainability

maintain or improve certain processes
or systems over the long term
without depleting resources

Infrastructure

Infrastructure refers to the fundamental physical and
organizational structures and facilities
needed for the operation of
a society or enterprise.

Empowerment

giving individuals or groups the tools, confidence,
and resources they need to take control of
their lives, make decisions,
and create positive change.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

I strongly believe that we can create a poverty-free world, if we want to. We can create a world where there won.t be a single human being who could be described as a poor person. In that world, the only place you would find poverty is in the museum. When school children will take a tour of the poverty museums, they would be horrified to see the misery and indignity that human beings once experienced. They would blame their forefathers for tolerating this inhuman condition which existed for so many people for so long.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
